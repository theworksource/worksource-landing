Questions

The basics, answered

What is WorkSource?

WorkSource is a verified hiring marketplace. Both job seekers and employers verify who they are, so every connection starts with trust, and both sides find real fit before anyone wastes time applying or interviewing.

What does it cost?

Job seekers are free, always. Employers are free too: every employer gets one active job post, free forever, enough to post a role, review verified candidates, and make a hire without paying a cent. Paid plans are only for employers who want several roles open at the same time. It's a real free account, not a trial.

How does matching work?

You tell WorkSource what's true: job seekers build one verified profile, and employers post a role and set the requirements that actually matter. WorkSource uses that verified information to surface stronger-fit matches before the application starts. The detailed matching logic is proprietary, but the experience is simple, you spend your time on real fits, not noise.

What does "verified" mean?

Identities are verified on both sides, and work history is confirmed rather than self-reported. Verification is built to reduce fake profiles, scam postings, and resume spam, so the people and roles you see are real.

Do I have to be in Charlotte?

WorkSource is launching in Charlotte first and opening city by city. You can join the waitlist from anywhere, we'll let you know the moment your area opens.

Why should my profile or job post be complete and accurate?

Job seekers: a complete, truthful profile helps surface the roles where you actually meet the employer's requirements. Employers: clear, specific requirements help WorkSource surface stronger-fit candidates, so you review fewer, better applicants.
